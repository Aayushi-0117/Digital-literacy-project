- Check Email & Message Authenticity
Verify sender addresses and avoid clicking on suspicious links or attachments.
- Use Strong, Unique Passwords
Combine letters, numbers, and symbols; never reuse the same password across accounts.
- Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
Add an extra security layer to email, social media, and banking apps.
- Be Careful on Public Wi-Fi
Avoid logging into banking or UPI apps on unsecured networks.
- UPI Safety – Verify Recipient Details
Double-check UPI IDs or phone numbers before sending money. Don’t rely only on display names.
- UPI Safety – Reject Unknown Requests
Never approve unsolicited “collect request” notifications or share your UPI PIN.
- Limit Personal Information Sharing
Avoid posting sensitive details like phone numbers, addresses, or college IDs on social media.
- Keep Devices & Apps Updated
Regularly update operating systems and apps to patch security vulnerabilities.
- Stay Alert to Scams
Be cautious of offers that sound “too good to be true” (lotteries, cashback, prizes).
- Report Cybercrime Immediately
Use the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) or call the Helpline 1930 to report fraud, cyberbullying, or identity theft.
